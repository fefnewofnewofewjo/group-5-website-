The hydrosphere includes all water on earth such as groundwater, oceans, rivers, lakes, ponds, glaciers, sea ice, and water vapor. Sometimes glaciers and sea ice are considered a part of the cryosphere. Many consider the cryosphere a subsection of the hydrosphere because it is still frozen water on the ground or floating on water.



The hydrosphere is essential for all life on Earth. Cells need water to function and maintain homeostasis. Earth's surface is approximately 71% water. Of all Earth's water, 96.5% is salty ocean water. The rest of Earth's water is freshwater lakes, rivers, glaciers, aquifers, and water vapor.

The total water on Earth is about 332.5 million cubic miles, or 1,386 million cubic kilometres (km3).
